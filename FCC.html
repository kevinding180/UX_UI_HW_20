FCC Redesign

A redesign of the Federal Communications Commission (FCC) government website was undertaken to improve the user experience. Research and testing identified key pain points in the existing site, and a redesign strategy was developed. User-centered design principles were utilized to create a modern and intuitive interface. Navigation, accessibility, and the ability to find information were improved. A responsive layout and clear, concise information architecture were implemented. As a result, engagement and satisfaction were improved.

Define the problem: In this step, we need to identify the key challenges with the existing website that need to be addressed. This can be done by conducting a website audit, reviewing user feedback, and analyzing website metrics. The key problems identified were: outdated design, unclear navigation, and lack of personalization.
Conduct User Research: The goal of user research is to gain insights into user behavior, needs, and pain points. We conducted a variety of research methods including surveys, interviews, and usability testing. The research revealed that users had difficulty finding the information they were looking for, were overwhelmed by the amount of content, and found the website difficult to navigate.
Create User Personas: Based on the insights gained from the user research, we created user personas to represent the different user types and their needs. This helped us to prioritize design decisions and ensure that the website design was tailored to the needs of our target audience.
Develop Information Architecture: We used the insights gained from the user research to develop a new information architecture for the website. This involved restructuring the navigation to make it more intuitive and creating a hierarchy of content to make it easier for users to find the information they need.
Create Wireframes and Prototypes: Wireframes and prototypes were created to test the new design concepts and gather feedback from users. We iteratively refined the design based on the feedback received until we arrived at a design that met the needs of our target audience.
Design and Development: The final step was to design and develop the website based on the approved wireframes and prototypes. The website was designed to be modern, user-friendly, and accessible, with clear navigation and personalized content based on user preferences.
